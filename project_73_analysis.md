## PHYSICAL IMPLAUSIBILITY ISSUES

### 8. Footprints on Wooden Deck in Rainstorm
**Severity:** MAJOR - Physically impossible

- Lines 287-305: Detective finds "distinctive marks" (footprints) on "soft, damp wood of the deck" during active storm with "swirling winds and rain"
- **Reality:** Wooden deck planks on ocean liners are treated/varnished and would not show footprints. Rain would wash away any marks immediately. Wind would disperse water constantly
- **Also:** If the wood is "soft" enough to show footprints, it's rotting and unsafe - this contradicts "luxurious passenger liner"
- This is the PRIMARY evidence linking Maxwell to the crime scene - but it's physically impossible

### 9. Metal Insignia "Buried in Wood"
**Severity:** MAJOR - Physically impossible

- Lines 315-317: "small piece of metal, partially buried in the wood"
- **Reality:** A metal insignia piece (thin decorative metal) cannot become "buried" in hardwood deck planking. It would bounce off or lay on top
- Would need massive force (like being shot from a gun) to embed metal in hardwood
- If there was a "struggle" as suggested, humans cannot generate enough force to embed metal in wood
- This is the SECONDARY evidence - also physically impossible

### 10. Storm Timing Impossibility
**Severity:** MODERATE - Physical constraint failure

- Line 287: "The night had descended" and "stormy weather had begun to brew"
- Line 294: Detective finds footprints on deck during "swirling winds and rain"
- **Problem:** If storm is active enough to have "swirling winds and rain," any footprints would be obliterated within seconds
- If detective is working in these conditions, how is she examining "soft, damp wood" carefully?
- Detective would be unable to conduct detailed forensic examination in active storm conditions

### 11. Evidence Preservation Failure
**Severity:** MODERATE - Police procedure failure

- Maxwell commands crew to "secure the crime scene" (line 35)
- Yet hours later, detective is discovering footprints on exterior deck in rainstorm?
- No crew member noticed obvious footprints leading to captain's quarters before this?
- **Issue:** Crime scene contamination would be massive; any real evidence would be compromised
- Basic crime scene procedures are ignored
